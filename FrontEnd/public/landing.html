features

a small set of things, done carefully.

no kitchen-sink editor. no folders. just notes, connections, and the tools to make sense of them.

01 · graph

see the shape of what you know.

every note is a node. every prerequisite is an edge. the graph view lays your notes out by depth — foundational at the top, derivative below — so you can see at a glance what the bedrock is and where you've built upward.

  • force-directed layout with depth levels
  • filter by tag, hover to focus a subgraph
  • drag, zoom, pan — it's just yours to explore

how it works

three steps to a thinking graph.

01

write a note.

a topic, a body, optional tags. that's it. no folders, no templates to choose, no syntax to memorize.

02

connect prerequisites.

mark which notes a note depends on. the graph emerges automatically — depth, structure, gaps and all.

03

study, search, ask.

generate flashcards, browse the graph, or ask the assistant questions grounded in your own notes.
